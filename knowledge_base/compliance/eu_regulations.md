# EU Import Regulations for Indonesian Spices

## COUMARIN IN CASSIA CINNAMON — EU REGULATION

### Regulation
- **EU Regulation (EC) No 1334/2008** (flavourings regulation)
- **EFSA Opinion (2004, 2008)**: Coumarin TDI = 0.1 mg/kg body weight/day
- Cassia cinnamon (C. burmannii, C. cassia, C. loureiroi) has very high coumarin content
- Ceylon cinnamon (C. verum) has negligible coumarin — fully safe

### Maximum Levels in Food
| Food Category | Max Coumarin (mg/kg) |
|--------------|---------------------|
| Breakfast cereals | 50 |
| Fine bakery (gingerbread, etc.) | 50 |
| Traditional/seasonal bakery | 50 |
| Breakfast cereals incl. muesli | 20 |
| General food (not seasonal) | 2 |

**Exporters must disclose**: Whether cinnamon is Cassia or Ceylon; buyers in EU may require certificate.

## PESTICIDE RESIDUES (MRLs)
- EU Regulation (EC) 396/2005 sets maximum residue limits (MRLs)
- Key pesticides monitored in spices: chlorpyrifos, DDT, endosulfan, aflatoxins
- COA must include pesticide residue testing by accredited laboratory

## AFLATOXINS
- EU Regulation 1881/2006 and amendments
- Aflatoxin B1: max 5 μg/kg in spices; Total aflatoxins (B1+B2+G1+G2): max 10 μg/kg
- OTA (ochratoxin A): max 15 μg/kg in spices
- Critical for nutmeg, pepper, chili — test mandatory for EU market

## MICROBIOLOGICAL STANDARDS
- Salmonella: Absent in 25g sample (Regulation 2073/2005)
- E. coli: < 100 CFU/g in most spices
- Fumigation requirements: EU restricts methyl bromide; phosphine alternatives accepted

## IMPORT ENTRY REQUIREMENTS INTO EU
1. Import notification via TRACES system
2. Health Certificate / Phytosanitary Certificate
3. COO from Indonesia (Form A for GSP, or FORM D for ASEAN FTA)
4. COA from accredited lab (ISO 17025)
5. For organic: EU Organic certificate (Control Union, Ecocert, etc.)

## GCC (Gulf Cooperation Council) REQUIREMENTS
- HALAL certification mandatory (by approved body — MUI for Indonesia recognized)
- Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait, Bahrain, Qatar, Oman
- Labeling must include Arabic, country of origin, production/expiry date
- Pesticide MRLs follow Codex Alimentarius + additional GCC standards

## US FDA (Food Safety Modernization Act — FSMA)
- Foreign Supplier Verification Program (FSVP) — US importers must verify suppliers
- Bioterrorism Act registration with FDA required for all food exporters to USA
- Prior Notice to FDA before each shipment
- Pesticide residues per EPA standards (stricter in some cases than EU)
