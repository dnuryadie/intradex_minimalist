# Incoterms 2020 — Complete Guide for Indonesian Spice Exporters

## OVERVIEW
Incoterms (International Commercial Terms) define the responsibilities of buyers and sellers in international trade. 
Published by the International Chamber of Commerce (ICC). Current version: Incoterms 2020.

## KEY INCOTERMS FOR INDONESIAN SPICE TRADE

### FOB — Free On Board
- **Full meaning**: Free On Board (named port of shipment)
- **Most common for Indonesian spices**: FOB Tanjung Priok, FOB Tanjung Perak, FOB Belawan, FOB Makassar
- **Seller's responsibility**: Deliver goods to the port, clear export customs, load onto vessel
- **Buyer's responsibility**: Ocean freight, marine insurance, import customs, inland delivery
- **Risk transfer**: When goods pass ship's rail at origin port
- **Use case**: Suitable when buyer has strong shipping relationships or freight contracts

### CIF — Cost, Insurance and Freight
- **Full meaning**: Cost, Insurance and Freight (named port of destination)
- **Seller pays**: Export customs + ocean freight + marine insurance
- **Buyer pays**: Import duties, inland delivery at destination
- **Risk transfer**: When goods are loaded on vessel at origin port
- **Note**: Seller must obtain minimum insurance (ICC Clause C)

### CFR — Cost and Freight
- **Full meaning**: Cost and Freight (named port of destination)
- **Similar to CIF** but seller does NOT pay insurance
- Buyer arranges their own marine insurance

### EXW — Ex Works
- **Full meaning**: Ex Works (named place, usually seller's warehouse)
- Seller's minimum obligation: make goods available at their premises
- Buyer handles everything: loading, export customs, freight, insurance, import

### DDP — Delivered Duty Paid
- **Full meaning**: Delivered Duty Paid (named place of destination)
- Seller's maximum obligation: delivers goods to buyer's named location, import duties paid
- Not recommended for most Indonesian SME exporters (complex import duty knowledge required)

### DAP — Delivered at Place
- Seller delivers to named destination but buyer pays import duties
- Good middle ground between CIF and DDP

## INDONESIAN EXPORT PORTS & TYPICAL FOB ROUTES

| Port | City | Primary Commodities |
|------|------|---------------------|
| Tanjung Priok | Jakarta | All major spices, consolidated cargo |
| Tanjung Perak | Surabaya | East Java spices, nutmeg transit |
| Belawan | Medan | Cassia, black pepper, rubber |
| Makassar | South Sulawesi | Clove, cocoa, vanilla |
| Ambon | Maluku | Nutmeg, mace, clove |
| Bitung | North Sulawesi | Clove, nutmeg, coconut oil |

## PRICE COMPONENTS IN FOB CALCULATION

FOB Price = Raw Material Cost + Processing Cost + Packaging Cost + 
            Domestic Freight to Port + Port Handling + Documentation + Profit Margin

## TYPICAL EXPORT DOCUMENT REQUIREMENTS

1. Commercial Invoice (CI)
2. Packing List
3. Bill of Lading (B/L) or Airway Bill (AWB)
4. Certificate of Origin (COO)
5. Phytosanitary Certificate
6. Certificate of Analysis (COA)
7. Fumigation Certificate (if required)
8. HALAL Certificate (if required by buyer)
9. Letter of Credit (if L/C payment terms)
