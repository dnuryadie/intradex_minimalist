# Export Documentation Guide for Indonesian Spices

## 1. COMMERCIAL INVOICE (CI)
- Issued by: Seller/Exporter
- Contains: Description, HS code, quantity, unit price, total value, Incoterm, payment terms
- Used for: Customs valuation, import duty calculation

## 2. PACKING LIST
- Details: Number of packages/units, weights (net/gross/tare), dimensions
- Must match Commercial Invoice exactly

## 3. BILL OF LADING (B/L)
- Issued by: Shipping line/carrier
- Types: Clean B/L, Charter Party B/L, Straight B/L, Order B/L
- Functions as: Receipt, title document, contract of carriage
- Negotiable B/L: Can be transferred (used in L/C transactions)

## 4. CERTIFICATE OF ORIGIN (COO)
- **Form B**: Standard COO issued by KADIN or Ministry of Trade
- **Form D**: ASEAN FTA preferential (for ASEAN destinations — zero duty)
- **Form A**: Generalized System of Preferences (GSP) for EU/USA
- **Form E**: ACFTA (ASEAN-China FTA)
- **Form AK**: ASEAN-Korea FTA
- **Form AI**: ASEAN-India FTA
- **Form AJ**: ASEAN-Japan FTA (AJCEP)
- **Form IJEPA**: Indonesia-Japan EPA

## 5. PHYTOSANITARY CERTIFICATE
- Issued by: BSIP (Badan Standardisasi Instrumen Pertanian) — Agricultural Quarantine
- Required by: ALL destinations for plant-based spices
- Content: Confirms product free of pests and diseases
- Valid: Usually 21 days from issue

## 6. CERTIFICATE OF ANALYSIS (COA)
- Issued by: Accredited laboratory (ISO 17025)
- Tests: Moisture content, volatile oil %, ash content, heavy metals, pesticide residues, microbiology
- Must state: Sample identification, test methods (AOAC, ISO, etc.)

## 7. FUMIGATION CERTIFICATE
- Required when: Wooden packaging material or pallets used (ISPM-15)
- Methods: Methyl bromide (restricted), Phosphine (accepted), Heat treatment
- EU/USA: ISPM-15 compliance mandatory for wooden pallets/crates

## 8. HALAL CERTIFICATE
- Issued by: MUI (Majelis Ulama Indonesia) — globally recognized
- Required for: GCC countries, Malaysia, some EU markets
- Valid: Usually 2 years

## 9. HEALTH CERTIFICATE / SANITARY CERTIFICATE
- Issued by: BPOM (National Agency of Drug and Food Control) or Ministry of Agriculture
- Required for: Processed spice products, extracts, oils

## 10. WEIGHT CERTIFICATE
- Issued by: Surveyor (SGS, Sucofindo, Intertek, Bureau Veritas)
- Confirms: Actual net/gross weight upon loading

## LETTER OF CREDIT (L/C) DOCUMENTATION CHECKLIST
When payment is by L/C, ensure all documents comply EXACTLY with L/C terms:
- Commercial Invoice: name/address must match L/C beneficiary
- B/L: must be "Clean on Board" and within L/C shipment date
- All documents: must be presented within L/C validity
- CRITICAL: Any discrepancy may result in payment refusal — use trade finance consultant

## INDONESIA EXPORT PROCEDURES STEP BY STEP
1. Export License (NIB — Nomor Induk Berusaha from OSS system)
2. Receive Purchase Order from buyer
3. Arrange goods, packaging, fumigation if needed
4. Book vessel/container with freight forwarder
5. Submit export declaration (PEB — Pemberitahuan Ekspor Barang) to Customs (DJBC)
6. Pay export duties if applicable (most spices: 0% export duty)
7. Load goods, get B/L from shipping line
8. Obtain all supporting documents (COO, Phytosanitary, COA)
9. Present documents to bank for L/C negotiation or send to buyer (T/T basis)
